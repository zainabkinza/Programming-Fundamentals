Paradox
Irony
Metaphor

Q3. The word “ephemeral” most nearly means:
Everlasting
Momentary
Dull
Permanent
B
Hint: Something that lasts only a short time.
---
Q4. Which of the following sentences contains a dangling modifier?
After reading the original study, the article remained unconvincing.
After reading the original study, I found the article unconvincing.
The article was unconvincing after I read the study.
I found the article unconvincing after reading the study.
A
Hint: The subject doing the action is missing.
---
Q5. In poetry, enjambment refers to:
The repetition of consonant sounds
A pause at the end of a line
The continuation of a sentence beyond a line break
A deliberate rhyme scheme
C
Hint: When a poetic line flows into the next without pause.
---
Q6. The word “pedantic” describes a person who:
Is excessively concerned with minor details
Avoids intellectual discussions
Is unpredictable and moody
Is generous with praise
A
Hint: Someone who corrects your grammar in casual talk.
---
Q7. Which of these sentences uses the subjunctive mood correctly?
If I was you, I’d take the offer.
If I were you, I’d take the offer.
If I am you, I’d take the offer.
If I will be you, I’d take the offer.
B
Hint: Subjunctive mood is for imaginary or unreal situations.
---
Q8. In literary theory, intertextuality means:
Comparing authors from the same era
The relationship between different texts
The author’s personal influence on writing
The text’s relation to historical context
B
Hint: When one text echoes or references another.

Loquacious – Reticent

Verbose – Garrulous

Hint: Both mean talkative or using too many words.

Metaphor
